H0: There is no difference in average housing starts between high interest rate months and low interest rate months

H1: Average housing starts are lower during high interest rate months than during low interest rate months.

My project will look at whether higher interest rates are associated with lower housing market activity in the United States. I will use monthly data from the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis FRED database, including the federal funds rate. My hypothesis is that months with higher interest rates will have lower average housing starts. I will use bootstrapping to estimate a confidence interval for the difference in average housing starts between high interest rate months and low interest rate months. I will also create visualizations such as time series plots and boxplots to show the relationship between interest rates and housing activity.
